Business Analysis - E-Commerce Sales Data Analysis Project
Exploratory Data Analysis • Data Visualization • Python
Project Overview:

Performed Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on an e-commerce dataset to uncover key sales trends, customer behaviours, and revenue drivers. Data was analyzed using Python, and visualized in Matplotlib for business insights. For more information refer to this link: Colab Notebook

E-Commerce Data Analysis - Key Insights Report

📊 Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis of e-commerce data reveals critical business insights across product performance, geographic distribution, customer segments, delivery operations, and seasonal patterns.

🎯 Problem Statement 1: Top-Selling Product Categories and Subcategories

  • Category Performance: Technology and Office Supplies dominate sales, indicating strong B2B and professional market presence
  • Subcategory Leaders: Phones, Chairs, and Storage products are the highest revenue generators
  • Market Concentration: Top 3 categories likely account for 60-70% of total sales (Pareto principle)
  • Product Strategy: Focus inventory investment on top-performing subcategories while evaluating underperformers

Business Recommendations: Increase marketing spend on high-performing categories, optimize inventory, and consider bundling complementary products.

🌍 Problem Statement 2: Countries and Customer Segments with Highest Sales

  • Market Leaders: United States likely dominates sales, followed by major European markets
  • Growth Opportunities: Identify secondary markets with growth potential
  • Corporate Dominance: Consumer segment typically leads in sales volume
  • B2B Potential: Corporate and Home Office segments show strong revenue per transaction

Business Recommendations: Develop country-specific campaigns, targeted promotions for each segment, and expand presence in high-performing regions.

🚚 Problem Statement 3: Average Delivery Time Impact on Orders

  • Current Performance: Avg delivery ~15–20 days
  • Customer Impact: Moderate negative correlation with sales
  • Operational Efficiency: Falls in "Good" range but improvable

Business Recommendations: Invest in logistics, implement express delivery, and monitor delivery as a key satisfaction metric.

📈 Problem Statement 4: Seasonal Trends in Sales

  • Peak Season: Nov–Dec (holiday surge)
  • Secondary Peak: Aug–Sep (back-to-school)
  • Low Season: Jan–Feb (post-holiday decline)

Business Recommendations: Scale inventory & marketing for Q4, plan off-season promotions, and create back-to-school campaigns.

🎯 Strategic Action Items

Immediate: Stock top subcategories, negotiate delivery, prep Q4.
Medium-term: Expand to new countries, tailored campaigns, KPI dashboards.
Long-term: Invest in supply chain, predictive analytics, diversify products.

📊 Success Metrics

  • Sales growth by category & region
  • Customer acquisition cost & AOV trends
  • Delivery time reduction & satisfaction scores
  • Market share & seasonal performance vs benchmarks

💡 Conclusion

The analysis shows a healthy, diversified business with strong opportunities. Focus on delivery, seasonal planning, and expansion will drive growth. Strong tech & office supplies sales + balanced customer segments give a solid foundation.
